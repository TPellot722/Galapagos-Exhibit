THE GALAPAGOS ISLANDS

The Galápagos Islands (official name: Archipiélago de Colón), part of the Republic of Ecuador, are an archipelago of volcanic islands distributed on either side of the equator in the Pacific Ocean, 906 km west of continental Ecuador

The Islands are known for their large number of endemic species and were studied by Charles Darwin during the second voyage of the HMS Beagle

The islands and their surrounding waters form the Galápagos Province of Ecuador, the Galápagos National Park, and the Galápagos Marine Reserve. The principle language on the island in Spanish, and the islands have a population of slightly over 25,000

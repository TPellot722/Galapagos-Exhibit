Reptiles

Lava Lizard

Small, quick lizards that eat mostly insects. They are territorial, and can often be seen doing "push-ups" to mark their territory

Galapagos Tortoise

Perhaps the most famous of all Galapagos species. they mostly live in the highlands and can be found on multiple islands

Marine Iguana

Marine iguanas feed underwater, gnawing algae off rocks that are sometimes as deep as 15 meters (50 feet). They are common throughout the islands.

Birds

Blue-footed Booby

Named for their vibrant, sky blue webbed feet, they are the favorite birds of many travelers. they can be found at the shoreline of most islands.

Flightless Cormorant

The only cormorant species in the world that has lost the ability to fly. With only 1500 estimated individuals, it is one of the world's rarest birds.

Galapagos Penguin

Designed for frigid arctic conditions, it somehow adapted enough to be able to live at the Equator, and is in fact the only siecies of penguin that lives north of the Equator in the wild.

Birds

Dolphin

Several species of dolphins make their home in the Galapagos. The most commonly sighted dolphin species is the Bottlenose(pictured). The common dolphin, Risso's dolphin, and Striped dolphin also make their home here.

Whale

The whale most commonly spotted by the islands is the Bryde's Whale.

Galapagos Sea Lion

Galapagos Sea Lions are descended from ancestors who lived off the South American coast: a fierce storm likely brought them to the Galapagos, where they thrived.

Marine Life

Shark

A number of shark species call the Galapagos home. The most commonly seen is the White-Tipped Reef Shark, which is harmless. Less commonly seen are the endemic Galapagos shark, hammerhead shark, and whale shark.

Green Sea Turtle

Green Sea Turtles live and breed on the islands. They are a common sight for snorkelers, as they often feed in fairly shallow waters, eating jellyfish and gnawing seaweed and algae off rocks.

Ray

There are four different species of ray in the Galapagos: Golden Cownose Ray, Stingray, Spotted Eagle Ray, and Manta Ray. The most commonly sighted species is the Stingray.
